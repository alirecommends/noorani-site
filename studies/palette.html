Accessibility & UI

Creating an accessible 10-color palette for color-blind users

Designing for inclusivity at Rakuten by ensuring complex marketing view dashboards are accessible to merchants with weak eyesight or color blindness.

Project Objectives

The Scope

Create a cohesive palette of 10 distinct colors specifically for the marketing view dashboard at Rakuten.

Target Users

To be used by a wide range of merchants from all demographic groups, specifically showing different age groups for quick visual interpretation.

Primary Emphasis

Make the color palette fundamentally accessible for people suffering from weak eyesight or various forms of color blindness.

The Method

The initial palette was eyeballed through trial and error and repeatedly checked for color-blindness accessibility using the Color Blind plugin in Figma. But the random method proved inefficient and time-consuming.

1

Adjusting via HSL & HSB

Pairs were adjusted methodologically based on HSL (Hue, Saturation and Luminance) and HSB color systems (Hue-Saturation-Brightness) to build distinction between color pairs that looked similar in some color-blind tests.

2

Fine-Tuning the Channels

HSB proved very helpful in contrast building but was no longer enough to make fine adjustments. Red-blind tests still had trouble, so the Green and Blue channels were adjusted using the RGB system to make a definitive difference.

Finalizing RGB Adjustments

Achievements

  • Accessibility results are not perfect but very satisfactory given the constraints of a 10-color system.
  • Good contrast is achieved in the final dashboard charts.
  • Colors are clearly distinguishable in most demographic viewing scenarios.
  • Rearranging items to achieve the best next-to-next contrast and hue proved highly useful.

A Word of Caution

Even existing standard palettes used in subway maps cannot provide 100% accessibility for all color-blind people when it comes to palettes of 10 or more colors.

The red-blind is the weakness of this palette with two close pairs. The issue can be effectively minimized by setting one of the red-blind-weak pairs to the smallest fraction on the generated charts.

Conclusion & Takeaways

"Creating an accessible 10-element color palette seemed challenging at first -- and it was. Digging deep into a problem that most people do not seem interested to take on was very rewarding."

Overcoming Randomness: Understanding how color blindness works seemed daunting at first and it took a long time to come up with a system to tweak the palette. It seemed random and disappointing before I started working mathematically in HSL, HSB and RGB systems. Persevering and trying all options finally rewarded me with an acceptable solution.

Workflow Setup: Setting a comfortable layout to study and display the iterations in a way that is manageable was difficult initially due to the lack of prior precedent in this specific project type.

Continuous Improvement: There are many accessible palettes of up to 5 colors on the web, but 10 colors cause duplicate hues. This is definitely a work in progress that should be further challenged by everyone; experts and newcomers alike.
