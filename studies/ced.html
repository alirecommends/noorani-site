A New Home for the Canadian English Dictionary

Bridging a gap in Canada's linguistic resources by designing the first comprehensive, accessible digital dictionary tailored to Canadian English in over 20 years.

Role

Lead UI/UX Designer

Duration

5 Months

Team

4 Members

Partnership

Toronto Metropolitan Univ.

Project Objectives

Highlighting Canadianisms

Preserve and celebrate Canada's linguistic heritage by emphasizing regional language variations and markers of national identity.

Supporting Diversity

Create an inclusive resource to aid language proficiency for a growing, diverse demographic, specifically assisting integration and overcoming language barriers.

Science of Learning

Enhance engagement and retention of vocabulary through active learning, meaningful interaction, and dynamic educational tools.

Research & Discovery

Street Interviews & Competitive Analysis

We began with a video survey at Yonge and Dundas Square in Toronto, gathering authentic feedback on perceptions of Canadianisms. We coupled this with an in-depth analysis of existing dictionaries.

  • Pain Points Found: Inconsistent entries, disruptive layouts, and poor overall usability in competitors.
  • Key Insight: A participant from Newfoundland highlighted the critical need to design for localized, regional language differences.

Survey Outcomes: A Tale of Two Users

From ~40 extensive survey responses assessing feature preferences, a stark divide emerged regarding the entry page experience:

The Majority: Interactive

Desired a modern, feature-packed online experience. They wanted interactive tools to aid learning.

The Minority: Traditional

Preferred a simple, barebones layout without "bells and whistles"—similar to classic print dictionaries.

The Solution: Dual-Layout Strategy

To respect both user segments, I set out to provide not just what they wanted, but the autonomy to control their own experience. This approach drastically improved accessibility and inclusion.

Mode 01

Interactive Experience

Designed for users seeking a modern, feature-rich interface.

  • • Easy Scanning: Browse multiple meanings quickly.
  • • Engagement: Comments, Like, Save, and Share functions.
  • • Focus: Collapsible Meaning cards to isolate target connotations.
Mode 02

Compact Mode

For users preferring a traditional, minimalist approach.

  • • Uncluttered: Focuses strictly on the definitions.
  • • Accessibility: Larger default font sizes for visually impaired/older users.
  • • Familiarity: Mimics traditional print dictionaries.

Applying the Science of Learning

A dictionary is an educational tool. Researching the science of learning revealed that users found standard "Word Lists" static and hard to engage with, leading to high abandonment rates.

The Fix: I engineered dynamic filters and interactive functions for the favorite words list. This elevated user engagement and actively assisted users in recalling previously saved vocabulary.

Usability Testing & Iteration

Insight 1: Community Desire

Users wanted to discuss quirky meanings at length. This directly led to the implementation of the Comments Section, transforming the dictionary from a static resource into an interactive community hub.

Insight 2: Structural Hierarchy

Users were surprised when derived meanings became the primary definition. This prompted complex stakeholder discussions resulting in strict architectural rules regarding how meanings could be rearranged.

Future Enhancements

Advanced Search

Filters prioritizing Canadianisms to provide a targeted experience for researchers and educators.

Canadian French

Expanding the platform to support Canada's bilingualism goals, based on overwhelming public support.

Region-Specifics

Expanding the taxonomy to highlight localized linguistic variations across diverse communities.
